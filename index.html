Crop Yield and Climate Trends in California's Central Valley

This project analyzes crop yield sensitivity to climate stress (temperature and precipitation) across 15 counties in California's Central Valley from 2010 to 2024.

Key Finding

Corn yield drops by 26% in hot years. Barley shows an even more extreme 50% decline under temperature stress, while beans and wheat exhibit modest gains.

Data Sources

  • USDA NASS QuickStats (crop yield, acreage, production)
  • NOAA Climate Data Online (daily temperature and precipitation)
  • Census TIGER/Line county boundaries (GeoJSON)

Methods

  • Collected climate data from 15 representative stations using the NOAA API
  • Aggregated daily climate data into annual features (TMAX, TMIN, PRCP)
  • Paired with USDA crop data and trained Random Forest models per crop
  • Analyzed yield changes in hot years (>1 standard deviation above average temperature)

Visual Outputs

  • Per-crop yield models and feature importance plots
  • County-level maps showing hot-year impact on yield
  • Heatmap comparing climate sensitivity across crops
